How to write a comment in 3 steps

 

 

 

 

 

Hi there, let's take a closer look today at how to write a comment. So when students want to write comment they often struggle with these three steps the first one is finding a relevant position so one that really has to do with your topic and doesn't drift off towards another topic. The second one is finding arguments that are relevant for this position and really defend it. The third one is really writing the text as in writing a structured text.

Let's take a detailed look now at how to do that.

Let's go! "How to write a comment" Now, I think this is best talked about if you have an example so let me give you this example. Let's say you have read an article about how difficult it is to live as an undocumented immigrant in the United States today how these people are being deported from the Trump administration. Then you get the task: "Comment on the US government's changes in immigration policy". Now where do you go from here?

You have a lot to do but it's really easy if you follow three simple steps. Step one: find a valid position that means a meaningful position one position that has to do something with the task you were given. Let me give you some possible positions you could occupy here.

Roughly this would be these changes to the policy were on the whole more harmful so they're not good; or that you could focus on how they are not humane how they don't treat immigrants like humans; you could say they were necessary even though they're harsh; or you could say they are simply successful because they achieved their goal of lowering the number of immigrants. Before we focus on arguments as a next step let's look at some positions that are not valid so that are invalid for this task.

You will have students or I often had students that write something like this they will say "too much immigration is bad for a country" and this will be their sort of their main position that they will support with arguments .The problem here is this is not really what the task is asking you for. You're supposed to comment on the US government's changes in immigration policy not on immigration as such so this is off topic, yeah, it is not good. On the other side people could start off writing a text and just talking about how President Trump is not good and how he has made many mistakes and often is wrong on a number of things.

This might be true but it's not really on topic of changes in immigration policy, so do not pick a position like this, either.

All the other four positions you see there as examples they have something to do with the main topic. You need to find arguments of course to support these, your position cannot simply list the position and not have any arguments for it right. So if you're on the side that these are; if your line of argumentation focuses on that these changes are on the whole good, are beneficial, you could have arguments something lik

You could say these changes are good because it is necessary to know about the number of immigrants in the country and because these numbers cannot be too high because this will be a problem for institutions or there shouldn't be people in the country that the government doesn't know anything about because who knows who they are, right? And on the other side, on the other line of argumentation against these changes you could find the following arguments, for example: that these people have been working and paying taxes so why remove them that these deportations are really trouble not just for this one person but also for their family and neighbors and so forth and that it's much more important to integrate people than to deport them.

You know all of these arguments in themselves are valid and depending on what position you have you can pick them and add them to your line of argument. Last step, step 3, would be write your comment and now this sounds easy but of course there are some things you should keep in mind. First of all use paragraphs, don't just write one long text where I as a reader cannot see where we have a new idea start or where we have a main part or a conclusion or whatever; so use paragraphs to make that very clear.

There should be an introduction in this introduction you refer to the topic that you were given and directly you also present your position on this topic. In the main part you will list the arguments that we talked about before and because it's a comment you will pick arguments that represent your position but sometimes it's effective to pick an argument from the other side but then refute it; so say "some people might say so-and-so but really that is not true because.

.." to support your position. There should be a conclusion where you sum up the points you made you don't really list the arguments again or repeat them; the point is you maybe weigh them or you evaluate them and say "On the whole we can see it's much more important to this and this".

So you should really put something together instead of just repeating things.

This is very tricky but it's possible. So these really are the most important steps in writing a comment if you follow them your comments should be quite good. Have fun! if you have any other questions or comments on this video please let me know on our try to get back to you - goodbye!.
